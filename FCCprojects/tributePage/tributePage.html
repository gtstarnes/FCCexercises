Plotinus

Father of Neoplatonism

Timeline of Plotinus's life and ideas

  • He was born in the year 205CE in Lyco, Egypt.
  • He is believed to be Greek, as he was raised in the Greek tradition, spoke Greek, and recieved a Greek education.
  • He had a distrust in the physical world, believing it to be a poor mimicry of a higher, nonphysical reality.
  • As a result, he never wanted a portait (painting or bust) of himself made.
  • In the year 232 CE, he travelled to Alexandria to study philosophy.
  • He disliked every teacher he came into contact with until he heard Ammonius Saccas lecture. He promptly became Ammonius Saccas's student.
  • He studied in Alexandria for 11 years. Eventually he developed a interest in the philsophy of the Persians.
  • So, in 243 CE, he joined Gordian III's military campaign in Persia to get some exposure to their philosohy.
  • The campiagn was a failure, and he was ultimately trapped in Persia and had to negotiate he way back to the Roman Empire.
  • At the age of 40, he moved to Rome, where he stayed for the rest of his life teaching philsophy.
  • Some of his students include: Porphyry, Eustochius, and Castricuius Firmus.
